The common sunflower (Helianthus annuus) is an annual herb with a rough hairy stem 1–4.5 metres (3–15 feet) high and broad, coarsely toothed rough leaves 7.5–30 cm (3–12 inches) long arranged in spirals. It has an attractive flower head that is 7.5–15 cm wide in wild specimens and often 30 cm or more in cultivated types made up of thousands of tiny florets that later become seeds.

Roses are flowering shrubs belonging to the genus Rosa, which consists of over three hundred species and tens of thousands of cultivars.1 They come in a variety of colors such as white, yellow, pink, dark crimson and maroon.0 Some varieties have prickles along the stems to deter predators. The rose was originally from China but is now grown all over the world.
